TASK: Evolve a Group Persona from Descriptive to Predictive.

You are a behavioral anthropologist and persona architect.
Your job: extract the underlying professional norms this community operates on,
integrate them into group traits, and rewrite the group summary so it predicts
how this community posts on LinkedIn — not just describes what they post about.

---

INPUTS:
Current Group Persona: {current_group_persona_json}
Batch Error Logs: {batch_error_logs}

---

HOW TO READ BATCH ERROR LOGS

Each gap block contains:
- behavioral_gap: the move the prediction missed
- missing_nuance: specific trigger/condition absent from traits (or null)
- evidence: "X/Y posts"
- confidence: HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW
- exists_in_context: true | false

Processing rules:
- Read ALL gaps before making any changes
- exists_in_context: true → refine existing trait only, never add new
- exists_in_context: false → evaluate for new trait
- LOW confidence single batch → discard unless move is highly distinct
  and absent from all existing traits
- Cluster gaps describing the same underlying move into one update
- Do not create separate traits for the same behavior across different batches

BEFORE PROCESSING — filter these out immediately:
Any gap that describes format, length, tone, or style
(short replies, specific nouns, emoji use, post structure)
is not a behavioral gap. Discard before Phase 0.
Only gaps that reveal what this community believes or values proceed.

---

PHASE 0: NORM EXTRACTION (internal — do not output)

This is the most important phase. Everything else depends on it.
Do not start editing traits until this phase is complete.

Read all surviving gap blocks. For each cluster of related gaps:

STEP 1: What is the underlying professional norm?

A norm is what this community believes about professional communication,
credibility, or work — not what they do in one specific situation.

Test: Complete this sentence:
"This community believes [professional belief] —
which is why they consistently [move] rather than [what predictions assumed]."

The belief must:
- Hold across multiple situations, not just the one in the gap
- Be specific to this community, not generic to all tech professionals
- Explain the move without referencing the specific post topic

If you cannot complete the sentence → the gap is a surface observation.
Discard. Do not write a trait from it.

STEP 2: Is this norm already captured in existing traits?

Read every trait text AND rationalization in the relevant category.
Compare the norm, not the surface behavior.
- Same norm already rationalized → REFINE that trait's text and rationalization
- Same surface behavior, different norm → NEW trait
- Vague overlap → REFINE, never add

STEP 3: Which category?
- Skills & Expertise: what they demonstrably KNOW or CAN DO
- Working Style: HOW they approach work, methods, processes
- Motivations & Values: what DRIVES posting choices, what they stand for
- Pain Points & Needs: specific frustrations that surface in posts
- Org Leadership & Psychographic Profile: how they LEAD, connect, position themselves

STEP 4: Build your edit plan before touching anything.
- List every trait that needs refinement → edit in place
- List every genuinely new norm → new trait in correct category
- Maximum 10 new traits total across all categories
- Refinements are unlimited

STEP 5: Build the behavioral profile.
Synthesize all confirmed norms — from gap clusters AND existing trait rationalizations:

Which norms appear across multiple traits and multiple gap clusters?
→ Core beliefs. These define how this tribe communicates on LinkedIn.

Which norms appear in only one category or one gap cluster?
→ Situational. They activate in specific contexts only.

Write the profile:
"This community operates on [core norm 1] and [core norm 2].
Situationally, when [context], they also [norm 3]."

This profile is your test for every trait and every sentence in the summary.
If a trait or summary sentence cannot connect to a norm in this profile → cut it.

---

PHASE 1: REFINE EXISTING TRAITS (internal — do not output)

For each refinement from Phase 0 edit plan:

1. Find the exact trait object in the persona JSON.
2. Edit text in place — never add a duplicate row for a refinement.
3. Every trait text must read as a sharp, natural observation about 
   who this community is and how they think — not a conditional rule 
   or decision tree.

   Write like a perceptive analyst describing a person, not like a 
   system prompt describing a behavior pattern.

   WEAK: "Values mentorship and community building."
   STRONG:"They are deeply skeptical of borrowed authority — titles, employer 
prestige, and trend participation carry no weight here unless the 
underlying mechanism stands on its own."


   Test: read the trait aloud. 
   1. Does this tell me who these people ARE professionally?
   2. Does this tell me how they THINK about their work?
   3. Does this tell me what they STAND FOR or push back against?

Does it sound like something a sharp analyst would say about this community in a briefing?
   YES → keep
   NO → rewrite in plain descriptive language

   Length: 1-3 sentences maximum. If it needs more → it's two traits.
4. Rewrite rationalization — required for every refinement, no exceptions.

   The rationalization extracts the underlying norm the trait encodes.
   It is NOT a summary of the trait text.
   It answers: what does this community believe that makes this behavior consistent?

   Must be grounded in gap block evidence (behavioral_gap, missing_nuance, evidence count).

   WEAK: "Updated based on prediction errors."
   WEAK: Restating the trait text in different words.
   STRONG: "This community treats specific public attribution as credibility
   currency — gaps showed predictions assumed generic gratitude was sufficient,
   missing that naming who helped and what they did is how trust is earned
   here (HIGH, 4/5 posts across batches 3 and 7)."

   If text changed but rationalization is unchanged → invalid. Fix before continuing.

5. Raise confidence_score if gap evidence strengthens the trait.

---

PHASE 2: ADD NEW TRAITS (internal — do not output)

Only for norms confirmed as NEW in Phase 0 edit plan.
Maximum 10 new traits total across all categories.

For each new trait:

STEP A: Write text as a natural, descriptive observation about this 
community's professional beliefs or behavior.

Same format rules as Phase 1 Step 3 — reads like an analyst briefing, 
not a conditional rule.

The behavior or belief must be real and specific to this community.
Test: "Could this describe any tech professional community on LinkedIn?"
If YES → too generic. Rewrite or discard.

Length: 1-3 sentences maximum.
STEP B: Write rationalization.
One sentence. The professional norm driving this behavior.
Must trace directly to gap block evidence.
If not traceable → null. Do not invent.

STEP C: Set confidence_score.
- Repeated HIGH across multiple batches → 0.85–1.0
- MEDIUM or single HIGH batch → 0.65–0.84
- LOW → do not add. Discard.

STEP D: Set trait_section_title.
Match existing subsection title in that category if one fits.
New title only if behavior is categorically distinct.

STEP E: Set keywordTags.
Only tags genuinely new to this category. No duplicates.

STEP F: Integration check before finalizing.
Strip each candidate trait and each existing trait in the category to a one-sentence
core belief (remove conditional triggers, examples, and proper nouns). Compare cores only.
□ Core belief duplicates any existing trait in any category? → merge in place or discard
□ Right category — most actionable for prediction?
□ Trait text is analyst observation prose — NOT a "When they…" / "If they…" conditional rule?
□ Rationalization traces to gap evidence?
□ Norm connects to behavioral profile from Phase 0?
If any fail → fix or discard.

---

PHASE 3: REWRITE GROUP SUMMARY (internal — do not output)

The group summary must answer:
Who is this tribe and what do they believe about professional communication
on LinkedIn — such that someone could predict their next post?

STEP 1: List what stays.
Read current summary. Identify what is still valid:
organizations, ecosystems, technical domains, sub-cohorts, posting modes.
These are preserved unless gap evidence clearly supersedes them.

STEP 2: Test current summary against behavioral profile.
Take the behavioral profile from Phase 0 Step 5.
Read every sentence in the current summary.
Ask: does this sentence connect to a norm in the profile?
- YES → candidate to keep, sharpen, or reframe around the norm
- NO → candidate to cut unless it preserves essential specifics

STEP 3: Identify what's new.
What norms from the error logs are absent from the current summary?
Only add what traces to at least one trait rationalization.
If you cannot trace a behavioral claim to a rationalization → cut it.

STEP 4: Write the summary.

Rules:
- Full rewrite — not current summary with additions appended
- Opening sentence must differ from current summary
- Keep all still-valid specifics: named tools, platforms, sub-cohorts
- Every behavioral sentence must connect to a norm in the Phase 0 profile
- Behavioral norms expressed as tribe-level patterns — not post-level examples
- Length: **500–700 words** (soft target — complete sentences; never cut mid-sentence)
- Cut anything that could describe any tech professional community
- Cut vague themes: "values community", "emphasizes practical implications"
- Every sentence must pass: "Does this help predict how this tribe posts?"
- If named individual behavior is confirmed by traits → include once, not twice
The summary captures PERSONALITY and PROFESSIONAL IDENTITY —
not writing patterns or response formats.

Ask for every sentence:
"Does this tell me who these people are — what they care about,
what they believe, what drives them professionally?"
- YES → keep
- NO → cut, even if it's technically accurate

What belongs:
- What they value in their work and community
- What they believe about technology, leadership, careers
- What motivates how they show up professionally
- How they think about their own credibility and others'
- What they celebrate, critique, and stand for

What does not belong:
- How long their posts are
- What format they use
- How they structure replies
- Writing style, tone, emoji use
- Response patterns to specific stimulus types

A reader should finish the summary knowing who these people ARE —
not knowing how to imitate their LinkedIn formatting.

□ All format/style gaps discarded before Phase 0
□ Every norm in Phase 0 completes "This community believes [belief] —
  which is why they [move] rather than [prediction assumption]"
□ Every new and refined trait reads as a natural analyst observation —
  not a conditional rule, checklist, or decision tree
□ No trait ``text`` begins with "When they", "When the", "If they", or similar trigger phrasing
□ No trait exceeds 3 sentences
□ No trait reads like a system prompt or behavioral template
□ Every refined trait has a rewritten rationalization grounded in gap evidence
□ No rationalization restates the trait text or says "updated based on errors"
□ New traits ≤ 10 total
□ No refinement added as duplicate row — edited in place
□ No trait duplicated across categories
□ Confidence scores match gap evidence strength
□ Group summary is a full rewrite — opening sentence differs
□ Every behavioral sentence in summary connects to a norm in Phase 0 profile
□ No vague behavioral themes in summary
□ Named individual behavior appears once if present
□ Baseline specifics preserved: tools, platforms, sub-cohorts
□ No behavioral claim in summary without a supporting trait rationalization

If any check fails → fix before outputting.

Return ONLY valid JSON. No markdown. No commentary.
